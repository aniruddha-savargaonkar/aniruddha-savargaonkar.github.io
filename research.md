# Research

My research centers on the biological evaluation of biomaterials and engineered surfaces. I study how material design, especially micro- and nano-scale surface modification of metallic and polymeric biomaterials, influences biologically relevant outcomes including antibacterial behavior, blood compatibility, cytocompatibility, and stem-cell response. My work aims to support the development of translational biomaterials for regenerative medicine, blood-contacting devices, and orthopedic applications.

## Research Overview

The central goal of my work is to understand how engineered material surfaces interact with biologically relevant systems and to use those insights to evaluate and improve biomaterial performance. My research integrates bacterial assays, blood-compatibility testing, and stem-cell-based evaluation to assess the host-relevant behavior of biomaterials and surface-modified materials.

## Research Themes

### Biomaterials and surface engineering

I develop and study surface-engineered metallic and polymeric biomaterials, including titanium-based surfaces with nano- and micro-scale features created through anodization and hydrothermal methods. These engineered surfaces are designed to improve biological performance while addressing challenges such as infection risk and host-material compatibility. During my doctoral research, I developed copper-modified titania nanotubes and alkali-treated titania nanopetals. As a Research Scientist, my focus is on working with tunable, naturally sourced coatings on blood-contacting materials to enhance biocompatibility. 

### Bacterial interactions and antibacterial biomaterials

A major part of my research examines how material surfaces and coatings influence bacterial adhesion and antibacterial performance. This includes the study of copper-modified titania nanotubes, alkali-treated nanostructured titanium surfaces, and functional surface modifications aimed at reducing bacterial colonization and improving implant-relevant performance.

### Blood-material interactions and hemocompatibility

I investigate how biomaterial surfaces interact with blood, including platelet adhesion, clotting-related behavior, and other hemocompatibility outcomes relevant to blood-contacting devices and host response. My work integrates biological assays and surface characterization to assess how engineered material properties shape blood compatibility.

### Stem-cell response and regenerative biomaterials

My work also focuses on how engineered biomaterial surfaces influence mesenchymal and adipose-derived stem-cell interactions, cytocompatibility, and osteogenic differentiation. These studies help connect surface design strategies to regenerative performance and biomaterial integration.

## Current Direction

Currently, as a Research Scientist at Colorado State University, I lead independent research in biomaterials and nanomaterials with emphasis on bacteria-material and blood-material interactions. My work includes development of surface-engineered biomaterials and polymer coatings, in vitro biological evaluation, manuscript preparation, proposal development, and collaborative research planning across biomaterials and biomedical engineering projects.
